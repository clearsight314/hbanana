You know how when you write, you're supposed to think about your intended audience? Well, I don't have one in mind. I have absolutely no idea who you are, except someone who, by some means or another, has had the distinct misfortune of encountering my blog. In fact, I have only vague notions about what I want to write <i>about</i>, and they're sure as hell not cohesive. As you might imagine, that makes it rather difficult to know where to start. I suppose telling you what inspired me to create this directionless project is as good a place as any. 
<br>

A week or so ago I was going down a GitHub rabbit hole, as one does, and I found [my old robotics team's website](https://electricfrogies.github.io), another website I'd made for myself, and [a blog I'd made on Art of Problem Solving](https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c872668_the_clearsight_site). The earliest commits on those repositories are from mid-2016, which means I was nine years old at the time. Being nine years old, my writing was full of intensifiers, emojis, and exclamation points. My CSS left much to be desired (less neon would be my first request), and many of the things that I shared were things were well-known experiences or jokes that I happened to be encountering for the first time (because I was nine). And yet, these imperfect, silly little projects were earnest and they were me.
<br>

I like the concept of a personal blog because realistically almost no one is ever going to read anything I write here, but there is the idea that they <i>could</i>. It's different from a journal because it functions as an exercise in being earnest "in public" (heavy quotation marks there) but it's also not attention-seeking like Instagram or something. Rather than having something specific that I already want to convey, my hope is to use this blog for... whatever I want, with as little perfectionism as possible. (Hence the "no style please" theme; [riggraz](https://riggraz.dev)'s blog is pretty cool.) I do not know what it will all accumulate to. Guess we'll find out, won't we?
