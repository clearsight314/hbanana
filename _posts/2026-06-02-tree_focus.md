Being a baby undergrad with no car on Grounds (I know it's pretentious but it feels weird to say "campus" at this point) over the summer has been quite a different experience from the school year. In some ways, it's like when I was a little homeschooled kid; I definitely enjoy having the free time to read books of my choosing and do random projects like this. This is arguably better than that, though, because now I'm (ostensibly) an adult. My adventures are no longer limited to the immediate vicinity of my house. I can see movies, go bouldering, sneak into events for the free food-- y'know, explore the area.

One of my less exciting outings this weekend was a walk to the CVS on the Corner to pick up some hairties. It was nice out, maybe 75 degrees with a slight breeze, so on my walk back home I thought I'd sit on a bench in the shade for a bit, enjoy the scenery. When I took my glasses off to braid my hair (making use of those hairties I'd just purchased), the big tree I was looking at took on this neat bokeh quality. I spent the better part of half an hour trying to take a photo with this effect before my cousin, over text, gave me the idea to use my glasses lenses themselves. I don't think I quite got it-- a gouache painting or colored pencil sketch would probably be the best strategy if I had the materials-- but the results & process were fun nonetheless.
